Project Overview

The development assignment had two main objectives. First, students were asked to perform a competitive analysis of three different websites featuring a favoriting function. Second, we were tasked with implementing a similar website using React components and props, incorporating functionality features such as sorting, filtering, and aggregating. Throughout the project, I carefully analyzed three platforms to develop a competitive comparison chart, through which I learned how those platforms designed their 'liking,' sorting, filtering, and aggregating functionalities.Then, I used react to develop a simple webapp with similar functionality features.

Development

React, HTML, CSS, Next.js, and Vercel are used for development and deployment. Item data and images are mock data stored in a JSON file since backend implementation was not within the scope of the project.

After selecting the three platforms, general workflow of "favoriting" or saving items on each platform was examined resulting in the following competitive comparison chart below.

Competitive Comparison Chart

Chart Overview:

Overall, the user journey of "liking" or "saving" items on the three platforms differs based on what they are optimizing for. Specifically, YouTube and Spotify offer comprehensive sorting and collection workflows for users’ playlists which contain songs or videos. In contrast, Airbnb—focusing on the initial browsing and searching experience—has minimal sorting and customization options for personal "wishlists". For all three, the overall processes of liking or "saving" items are effectively identical; users select the like or save button and decide on the playlist or wishlist.

The most successful features arise from addressing users' primary needs for each platform. For Spotify, advanced sorting options within users' playlists (collections of liked songs) by mood and vibe—e.g., upbeat, classy, etc.—in addition to conventional sorting by artist name, album, and recent, enable users to quickly revisit their favorites that fit their current states(Optimizing for "F" skim pattern and idea of continuity). On the other hand, Airbnb offers a map view on the right side of the wishlist, which enables users to check proximity along with prices( connects back to proximity grouping principle), despite no sorting or search within the lists. For YouTube, the customizability and sorting functionalities lie between Spotify and Airbnb. However, YouTube’s user playlist's option to sort by popularity, recent, or manual sorting reflects the users' primary goal of consuming viral or relevant video content.

For Spotify, aggregated lists of podcasts lack advanced sorting, which can be inefficient when users save many podcast episodes. Similarly, Airbnb's lack of integrated sorting or search functionalities within their wishlists can result in a suboptimal user experience if users have liked or saved many candidates (although this could be intentional, coupled with the initial search and browsing experience).

My Development Goals & Inspirations

From the analysis, I quickly learned that sorting and filtering should be optimized for the user's main goal for the particular platform or service. However, sorting by fundamental attributes such as prices or ratings is an important requirement. By examining the commonalities as well as differences, I developed specifications that meet the project requirements, which are explained in detail in the next section.

Part2: Development

About Dave's Book Club

After performing the competitive analysis in part 1, I have learned about the fundamental features a platform needs to support aggregating, filtering, and sorting. Since the objective of the assignment is to utilize front-end skills (making use of props, components, and states), I have decided to develop a simple book listing website. On the website, visitors can view a list of books from my favorites as well as some bestseller books I found on Amazon.

To meet the functionality requirements, users can filter books based on two categories: book format (Kindle vs. paper) and source of recommendation (Dave's Favorites vs. Amazon Bestsellers). Users can also sort the list by various attributes such as publication date, price, and rating in ascending or descending order. Liking a book by pressing the heart icon will simply add the book to your favorites list. When one presses the 'add to cart' button, the cart aggregator will be updated.
